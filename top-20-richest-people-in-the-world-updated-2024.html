Top 20 Richest People In The World (Updated 2024)

According to Forbes, the top 20 richest people in the world have a combined net worth of over $1.7 trillion. The list is led by Amazon founder and CEO Jeff Bezos, who has a net worth of $193 billion. He is followed closely by Elon Musk, the CEO of SpaceX and Tesla, with a net worth of $180 billion.

Bernard Arnault, the CEO of luxury goods company LVMH, is in third place with a net worth of $150 billion. Bill Gates, the co-founder of Microsoft, is in fourth place with a net worth of $127 billion, while Facebook CEO Mark Zuckerberg is in fifth place with a net worth of $102 billion.

The top 20 richest people in the world come from a variety of industries, including technology, retail, finance, and luxury goods. Some of the other notable names on the list include Warren Buffett, the CEO of Berkshire Hathaway, Larry Ellison, the co-founder and CEO of Oracle, and Larry Page, the co-founder of Google.

Many of these individuals have made their fortunes through their own hard work and ingenuity, but they have also benefited from the growth of their companies and the overall strength of the global economy. As a result, their net worths have continued to grow, making them some of the richest people in the world.

Despite their impressive wealth, many of the richest people in the world are known for their charitable giving and philanthropic efforts. For example, Bill Gates and Warren Buffett have both pledged to give away the majority of their wealth through the Giving Pledge, a campaign that encourages the world’s wealthiest individuals to donate a significant portion of their money to charity.

In addition to their charitable work, some of the richest people in the world are also known for their business acumen and innovative ideas. Elon Musk, for instance, is known for his ambitious plans to revolutionize the transportation industry through the development of electric cars and reusable rockets.

Despite the impressive wealth of the top 20 richest people in the world, there is still a significant wealth gap between the richest and the poorest individuals around the globe. In order to address this issue, many experts believe that it is important for the world’s wealthiest individuals to continue to give back to their communities and support charitable causes that can help to reduce inequality and improve the lives of those in need.
