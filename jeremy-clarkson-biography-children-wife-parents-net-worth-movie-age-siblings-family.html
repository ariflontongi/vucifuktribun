Jeremy Clarkson Biography: Children, Wife, Parents, Net Worth, Movie, Age, Siblings, Family

Jeremy Charles Robert Clarkson is an English TV host, journalist, and author focusing on cars.

He gained fame for presenting Top Gear and The Grand Tour with Richard Hammond and James May. Additionally, he writes regular columns for The Sunday Times and The Sun.

As of 2018, he has hosted the ITV game show Who Wants to Be a Millionaire?

Early Life

Jeremy Clarkson was born on April 11, 1960, in Doncaster, England, to Shirley and Edward Clarkson.

Despite financial difficulties, his parents, who ran a tea cozy business, managed to send him to private schools.

His father, Edward, a travelling salesman, significantly impacted Jeremy’s passion for cars and adventure.

Additionally, Jeremy has a sister named Joanna Clarkson.

Education

Jeremy Clarkson’s educational journey was distinctive and a bit unconventional. It involved a mix of academic achievement and struggles with authority figures.

He first attended Hill House School in Doncaster, a preparatory school, and then later attended the prestigious boarding school Repton School.

Despite showing promise academically by obtaining nine O-levels, he faced challenges with the school’s rules and regulations.

Clarkson was ultimately expelled just before his A-levels for his behaviour, which included drinking, smoking, and causing disruptions. His A-level results ended with him receiving one C and two U grades.

Career

Jeremy Clarkson, a well-known English TV presenter and journalist, has had a diverse career over many years.

He began his journey by writing for local newspapers and car magazines, where he became recognized as a motoring journalist.

In 1988, he secured a presenting role on the original “Top Gear,” bringing his unique blend of humour and frankness to the show.

Clarkson also wrote columns for The Sunday Times and The Sun, garnering attention for his provocative yet captivating style.

In addition to “Top Gear,” he hosted several other TV programs, such as “Motorworld,” “Extreme Machines,” and “Clarkson,” before taking the lead on the revamped “Top Gear” in 2002.

This collaboration with Richard Hammond and James May turned the show into a global sensation, although it also drew criticism for its controversial jokes and scripted challenges.

Following a dispute with a producer, Clarkson’s dismissal from “Top Gear” in 2015 marked a significant moment in his career.

Subsequently, he joined forces with Hammond and May to create “The Grand Tour,” a motoring show on Amazon Prime reminiscent of their former series.

He later launched “Clarkson’s Farm,” a show centred around his farm, offering a more personal glimpse into his life.

Alongside his TV ventures, Clarkson continues to write columns and engage in public debates, known for sparking controversy with his viewpoints. Presently, he can be seen hosting the popular game show “Who Wants to Be a Millionaire?” on ITV.

Personal Life

Jeremy Clarkson is a twice-married man. Alex Hall was his first wife, and they were wed from 1989 to 1993.

Following his 1998 marriage to Frances Cain, the couple produced three children: Emily Clarkson, Finlo Clarkson, and Katya Clarkson. The couple divorced in 2014.

Ever since, Lisa Hogan and Clarkson have been romantically together. Among Clarkson’s many assets are an Oxfordshire, England farm and an apartment in London.

Clarkson is an avid car collector and enthusiast. He also likes to write, watch cricket, and farm.
