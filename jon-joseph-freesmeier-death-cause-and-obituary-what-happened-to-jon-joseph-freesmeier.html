Remembering Jon Joseph Freesmeier

News: On January 20, 2024, Jon Joseph Freesmeier, a skilled engineer hailing from Cedar Rapids, Iowa, peacefully departed this world, embraced by the love of his family. His passing has created an irreplaceable emptiness in the hearts of those fortunate enough to have known and admired him.

Early Life and Education

Jon Freesmeier, born on November 2, 1971, in Ft. Madison, Iowa, was the son of Delmar and Cheryl Freesmeier. He received his education at Marquette High School in West Point, IA, and later graduated with honors in mechanical engineering from the University of Iowa in 1995. Continuing his academic journey, Jon earned a master’s degree in engineering in 1997.

Engineering Career and Contributions

During his professional journey, Jon dedicated his expertise to notable companies, including TRW Automotive, Maytag, and Collins Aerospace. Throughout his tenure, he demonstrated exceptional engineering prowess, leaving an indelible mark with his contributions to the development of nine patents. His innovative work spanned diverse areas, from airbags and refrigerators to cockpit displays.

A Loving Family and a Passion for Travel

Jon and Michele McDermott joined their lives in matrimony on May 22, 1999, initiating a journey marked by love and adventure. Blessed with the joy of parenthood, the couple welcomed two children, Nathan and Claire, forming a close-knit family. Their shared enthusiasm for travel led them to explore new destinations together, creating cherished memories along the way.

Leaving Behind a Legacy

Jon is survived by his cherished wife, Michele, and their children, Nathan and Claire. Grieving his loss are his parents, Delmar and Cheryl Freesmeier, and his sister, Maria (Micheal) Beamer. The mourning circle extends to his mother-in-law, Maureen McDermott, brothers-in-law Jason (Dede) McDermott and Aaron McDermott, and sister-in-law Kristi (Gerry Notara) McDermott. Fond memories of Jon will also be held by his nieces and nephews: William Beamer, Sam, Rachel, Rebecca, and Anna McDermott.

Jon’s grandparents, James and Alice Mullahy, and Louis and Della Freesmeier, precede him in death. In his memory, grief is shared by his father-in-law, Ken McDermott, as well as several aunts, uncles, and cousins.

Funeral Arrangements

During this challenging period, the family kindly requests privacy and understanding. A visitation in memory of Jon will take place on Friday, January 26, commencing at 9:00 a.m. at St. Elizabeth Ann Seton Catholic Church in Hiawatha, leading to a funeral mass at 11:00 a.m. Subsequently, a private burial service will be held at Cedar Memorial Park Cemetery.

The passing of Jon Joseph Freesmeier is not only a profound loss to his family but also to the engineering community at large. His legacy will endure through the memory of his unwavering dedication, exceptional talent, and the meaningful impact he left through his work. May his soul find eternal peace.
