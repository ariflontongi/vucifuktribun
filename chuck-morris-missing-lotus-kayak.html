Lotus band member Chuck Morris &amp; son missing for a week after vanishing on spring break trip as haun

A MUSICIAN and his son are now presumed dead after they went missing on a kayaking trip last week.

Chuck Morris, the percussionist for the Colorado jam band Lotus, never returned from a wilderness trip in Arkansas with his son Charley.

Investigators no longer expect to find the two missing men alive, Lotus said in an Instagram post on Tuesday.

"We were all hoping for a miracle, but at this point, the search for Chuck and Charley has moved to a recovery," the band said.

"With help from the K9 team, there is a probable location on the lake, but logistical challenges including temperature, weather, and depth have prevented a recovery thus far."

The update came after police confirmed that they found the duo's kayaks near Beaver Lake last week, 9News reported. They also recovered a jacket belonging to one of the men.

Lotus has had a career spanning more than two decades. They're known for bringing electronic elements into the jam band genre, which is a type of rock music defined by improvisation.

The band formed at Goshen College in Indiana in 1998.

Although today the group's members are split between Denver and Philadelphia, its fanbase is particularly strong in Colorado.

In 2021, Lotus played the first show of the season at Colorado's iconic Red Rocks Amphitheater.

The group had just finished a month of touring before Chuck disappeared.

They were scheduled to go back on the road in a couple of weeks.

Despite the tragic news, they still plan to go on tour to honor Chuck's memory.

"While we are deeply grieving, we plan to celebrate Chuck and Charleyâ€™s lives, our memories with them, and what they meant to so many people," the band said in a statement.

Lotus encouraged fans to donate to the GoFundMe campaign set up to help Chuck and Charley's family.

It's already reached more than half of its $150,000 fundraising goal.

"Please know how grateful our family is for all the outpouring of support," Chuck's wife Jenny Thompson said in an Instagram post to the band's account on Monday.

"Please continue to hold my lovely husband and son in your hearts and prayers."

On Twitter, many Lotus fans shared tributes to the father and son.

"Rest in peace Chuck and Charley Morris," one said. "My heart goes out to their families, friends, and all of Lotus in this unbelievably heartbreaking time."

"We have been heartbroken to hear this news and are wishing desperately for a positive outcome to our dear friends in Lotus," they said.
