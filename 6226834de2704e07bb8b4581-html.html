Ukrainian neo-Nazi group lays claim to execution of Ukrainian MMA fighter Maxim Ryndovskiy

A neo-Nazi group in Ukraine has claimed responsibility for the execution of a Ukrainian athlete named Maxim Ryndovskiy on social media.

The wrestler is accused of having trained with Russian MMA fighters amidst the ongoing conflict between Russia and Ukraine.

"An athlete and idol of youth, a Jew, was shot," they say.

Ryndovskiy, a mixed martial arts fighter, was born in 1993, is a middleweight, weighs almost 84 kilograms and is 1.80 metres tall. He has one win and no defeats on his record. There is no further official communication, and it is not really known whether the fighter is still alive or not.

There is also a rumour circulating that the fighter had trained at a Chechen club. MMA Ahmat, which is very well known, has almost half a million followers on different social media and promotes great fighters from Russia.
