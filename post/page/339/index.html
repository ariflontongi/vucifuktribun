Jess Cagle — one of the highest-ranking, longest-serving and most well-liked magazine editors in the media business — is leaving his post as editor-in-chief of People magazine. Cagle, who also serves as editorial director of the entertainment group overseeing titles including Entertainment Weekly, will officially exit March 31 (the date his contract expires) after closing People’s April 8 issue. He announced the move Wednesday in a memo that was circulated among staffers and posted on EW’s website, a goodbye letter that’s a mix of personal revelations, professional achievements and a joke for good measure.
