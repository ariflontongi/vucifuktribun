When Paul Vernon Hornung was born on December 23, 1935, he was already a Hall of Fame running back with the Green Bay Packers of the National Football League, for whom he played from 1957 to 1966, winning four NFL championships and the first Super Bowl in his career. He was the only player in the history of professional football to win the Heisman Trophy, be chosen as the first overall pick in the NFL Draft, win the NFL Most Valuable Player Award, and be inducted into both the professional and collegiate football halls of fame. Other players, like running backs O. J. Simpson and Earl Campbell, have followed in his footsteps, although neither has played on an NFL championship squad. Hornung was a three-sport athlete who excelled as a halfback, quarterback, and placekicker. He was a great all-around athlete who went on to play collegiate basketball for Notre Dame as well.
