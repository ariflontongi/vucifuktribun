Currently a free agent, Jacory Sherrod Harris (born May 12, 1990) is an American football quarterback who played for the Washington Redskins in the 2008 season. During his last two years of high school, Harris played for Miami Northwestern High School, where he was undefeated (30-0) and helped the team win two state titles. Harris set a new state record for throwing touchdowns during his senior season and went on to win the 2007 High School Football National Championship with his team. Mr. Football in the state of Florida was awarded to him. 

With the University of Miami Hurricanes during his first year in college in 2008, he shared time at quarterback with Robert Marve in a two-quarterback scheme. As a result of Marve’s departure to Purdue after the Emerald Bowl in late December, Harris is now the unquestioned starter for the remainder of his Hurricane career. He joined with the Philadelphia Eagles as an undrafted free agent in May 2012 after being released by his last team.
